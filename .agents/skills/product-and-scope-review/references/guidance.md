# Guidance for Product and Scope Review

This skill borrows the strongest part of founder-mode planning without turning the repo into a persona pack.

## What To Produce

The output should hand cleanly into `spec-driven-development`:

- the real problem statement
- the chosen mode: `hold`, `expand`, or `reduce`
- the top assumptions that remain true
- the most important success criteria
- explicit non-goals when scope is being held or reduced

## How To Choose A Mode

- Choose `hold` when the request is already close to the right wedge and risk comes from hidden assumptions, not ambition.
- Choose `expand` when a slightly broader framing produces a noticeably better user outcome or removes obvious product traps.
- Choose `reduce` when the current ask is too broad, too risky, or packed with speculative features.

## Questions That Usually Matter

- What user pain or operator pain are we actually solving?
- What would make this request feel successful in real use, not just in code?
- What is the smallest version worth shipping?
- What tradeoff are we making on purpose by holding or reducing scope?

## Red Flags

- Debating implementation details before the user outcome is clear
- Expanding scope because the broader solution sounds impressive
- Reducing scope so aggressively that the remaining change no longer tests the real need
